# Cethos Audit-Readiness Sprint — 5-Week Implementation Plan

**Document status:** v0.1 — companion to roadmap v0.2
**Sprint window:** May 11 → June 12, 2026 (5 weeks of implementation)
**Buffer + audit:** June 15 → June 30, 2026 (weeks 6-7: mock audit, remediation, audit days)
**Hard rule:** The pharma sponsor is never named in any document, SOP, or external material. Standard phrasing: *"the late-June audit,"* *"the pharma sponsor's audit,"* *"the auditor."*

## 1. Team and roles

Three internal owners plus one external consultant. Roles are defined operationally (what each person does day-to-day) and audit-facingly (how each person appears in the audit org chart on June 29-30).

### Raminder Shah — Head of Quality / Owner

**Operational scope.** Strategic direction, final sign-off authority, consultant management, audit-facing executive. Authors policy-level documents (Quality Policy, Information Security Policy) and the SOPs requiring institutional context only he has (customer complaint handling, customer satisfaction monitoring). Owns external communications: consultant SOW, sponsor correspondence, marketing scrub. Reviews and approves every artifact before it goes into the audit dossier.

**Audit-facing.** Opens and closes the audit. Speaks to: company history, organizational structure, scope of certification activity, strategic quality direction, customer relationships, top-management commitment per ISO 9001 §5. Sits in on Fayza's operational sessions as backup, doesn't drive them.

**Sprint commitment.** ~75% of working time during the sprint. Continues business operations at reduced cadence.

### Fayza — Quality Manager (Operational)

**Operational scope.** Authors all production SOPs (forward translation, back translation, reconciliation, cognitive debriefing, harmonization, final QC) and all vendor management SOPs (qualification, onboarding, performance monitoring, re-qualification, offboarding). Owns the COA methodology master document. Identifies the qualified COA translator pool and works with Amrita on evidence collection. Delivers internal training to staff. Coaching from consultant on QA Manager audit-readiness is embedded throughout.

**Audit-facing.** Lead interviewee on every operational topic. Runs the document review sessions. Speaks to: vendor qualification, project workflow, revision/QA controls, translator selection logic, COA methodology, complaint and feedback handling at operational level. This is the role the auditor will probe hardest; Fayza needs to be fluent in every SOP, not just the ones she authored.

**Sprint commitment.** ~80% of working time. Translator scouting / vendor relations work continues at reduced volume; new vendor sourcing pauses except for COA pool gap filling.

### Amrita Shah — QMS Coordinator

**Operational scope.** Owns the document control system — SOP formatting, versioning, register, approval workflow, publication. Owns the records side: linguist credential collection and verification for the COA pool, NDA tracking, training record assembly, audit dossier compilation. Authors the systematic SOPs that are template-driven (document control, records retention, internal audit, CAPA, management review, and the information security control SOPs). Maintains the action tracker, decision log, meeting minutes. Coordinates audit logistics.

**Audit-facing.** Present at the audit as records coordinator. May be asked to demonstrate document retrieval and the qualification database queries. Not the primary interviewee on policy or methodology topics but expected to surface any document on request within seconds.

**Sprint commitment.** 100% of working time. This is the role most directly responsible for the audit not falling over on retrieval.

### External Consultant — QA Advisor

**Operational scope.** Gap analysis (Week 1-2), SOP review and ISO 17100 / COA-specific revisions (Weeks 2-5), ISMS-lite design (Weeks 3-5), Fayza coaching for QA Manager role (throughout), mock audit (Week 7), audit standby (Week 8 / June 29-30).

**Audit-facing.** Present during the audit as QA Advisor. Does not drive interviews. Available between sessions for real-time advisory.

**Engagement target.** SOW signed by May 19 at the latest.

## 2. Operating principles

Five rules that hold across the sprint.

**Daily standup at 09:00 MT, 15 minutes.** Three roles, three questions: what got done yesterday, what's planned today, what's blocked. Raminder leads. When Raminder is at NAJIT (June 5-7), Fayza leads.

**Friday retrospective at 16:00 MT, 30-60 minutes.** What worked, what didn't, what to change next week. The week's deliverables are reviewed against the plan; any slip is named and re-planned.

**Single source of truth: shared Drive folder `Cethos QMS — Audit Sprint 2026/`.** Subfolders: `01-policies/`, `02-sops-production/`, `03-sops-vendor/`, `04-sops-management/`, `05-sops-infosec/`, `06-records/`, `07-vendor-dossier/`, `08-audit-dossier/`, `09-tracking/`. No QMS document lives anywhere else. No "I'll email it" — file it, link it.

**Document register and action tracker, owned by Amrita.** Every SOP, policy, form, and register has a row in the document register with: code, title, version, owner, status (draft / in review / approved / published), last reviewed date, next review date. Every commitment from any standup or meeting goes into the action tracker with owner and due date. The auditor will ask to see both.

**No SOP is published without three signatures: author, reviewer, approver.** Author drafts; reviewer (typically the consultant for production/infosec SOPs, or Raminder for vendor/management SOPs) checks ISO conformance; approver (Raminder for policies, Fayza for SOPs) signs. Amrita publishes only after all three signatures are recorded.

## 11. Risks specific to this 5-week plan

Three risks worth tracking explicitly.

**Consultant lead time slips past May 19.** If the consultant isn't signed by then, Week 1's gap analysis is at risk and Week 2-3 SOP review backs up. Mitigation: keep three candidates active until SOW signed; if no qualified candidate at $15K by May 22, expand budget envelope rather than drop qualification bar.

**Fayza capacity squeeze.** Two jobs at 80% sprint commitment is realistic only if vendor scouting is genuinely paused except for COA pool gap filling. If she's pulled back into general scouting mid-sprint, SOP authoring slips. Mitigation: explicit weekly check on her actual time allocation in Friday retrospectives; escalate to Raminder if creep starts.

**Evidence gaps in the COA pool that can't be closed.** Some linguists won't respond, some can't produce credentials, some won't sign new NDAs. Mitigation: identify the gaps by end of Week 3; have Fayza decide pool removal in Week 4; document "qualified per CVP graduation + project history + reference check" as a compensating-control narrative for any specific evidence gap rather than dropping the pool member entirely.
