# Vendor Confidentiality Agreement — DRAFT v1

> **This is a draft for legal review.** It was authored by a non-lawyer to give your counsel a starting point. Do not present it to vendors until a qualified Canadian lawyer (ideally Alberta-licensed, with experience in services contracts and confidentiality) has reviewed and approved it. Notes for the lawyer are at the bottom of this file.

**Cethos Solutions Inc. — Linguist Confidentiality Agreement**

**Version:** 1.0-draft

## 1. Parties

This agreement is between:

- **Cethos Solutions Inc.**, a corporation incorporated under the laws of Canada, with its principal office in Calgary, Alberta (the **"Company"**); and
- the individual or sole proprietorship accepting this agreement through the Cethos Vendor Portal at the time and date recorded in the Company's systems (the **"Linguist"**).

The Company and the Linguist are each a **"Party"** and together the **"Parties."**

## 2. Purpose

The Linguist provides — or may from time to time provide — translation, revision, post-editing, transcription, interpretation, certification, or other linguistic services to the Company and its clients (the **"Services"**). In the course of providing the Services, the Linguist will receive access to information of the Company and of the Company's clients that is confidential or proprietary. This agreement sets out how the Linguist will protect that information.

## 3. Definition of Confidential Information

**"Confidential Information"** means any information disclosed to or accessed by the Linguist in connection with the Services, regardless of form (written, oral, electronic, visual) or whether marked confidential, including but not limited to:

(a) source texts, target texts, files, screenshots, and any subject matter of a translation, revision, or post-editing assignment;

(b) personal information of any natural person whose data appears in a source document, including names, contact details, financial details, health information, biometric data, immigration status, government identifiers, and any other personal information as defined under the *Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act* (Canada) (**"PIPEDA"**) and the *Personal Information Protection Act* (Alberta);

(c) translation memories (TMs), termbases, glossaries, style guides, and reference materials supplied by the Company or its clients;

(d) the identity of, and any information about, the Company's clients, prospective clients, suppliers, or sub-contractors;

(e) the Company's pricing, rate cards, payment terms, internal processes, software, vendor portal contents, vendor lists, business plans, and financial information;

(f) the existence, content, and terms of any assignment or instruction issued through the vendor portal; and

(g) any other information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

## 4. Exclusions

Confidential Information does **not** include information that the Linguist can demonstrate by contemporaneous written records:

(a) was lawfully in the Linguist's possession without obligation of confidence before disclosure by the Company;

(b) is or becomes publicly available through no act or omission of the Linguist;

(c) was lawfully received from a third party without obligation of confidence and without breach of any agreement; or

(d) was independently developed by the Linguist without use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

## 5. Obligations of the Linguist

The Linguist agrees to:

(a) **Hold in confidence.** Treat all Confidential Information as strictly confidential and exercise no less than a reasonable degree of care to protect it (and in any event, no less care than the Linguist applies to their own confidential information of similar nature).

(b) **Use restriction.** Use Confidential Information solely to perform the Services for the Company, and for no other purpose, including no personal, commercial, academic, or competitive use.

(c) **No disclosure.** Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party, including spouses, family members, colleagues, sub-contractors, or other linguists, except as expressly permitted in Section 6.

(d) **No reproduction.** Not copy, photograph, screenshot, transcribe, store, or otherwise reproduce Confidential Information except as strictly necessary to perform the Services, and only in secure storage controlled by the Linguist.

(e) **Secure handling.** Store all Confidential Information on devices that are password-protected and encrypted at rest; transmit Confidential Information only via channels approved by the Company (the vendor portal or Company-designated secure email); and not store Confidential Information on shared, public, or unsecured cloud services (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox folders shared with other parties, public file-sharing services).

(f) **Restricted use of artificial-intelligence tools.** Not submit, paste, upload, or otherwise transmit any Confidential Information to any public or third-party artificial-intelligence, machine-translation, or large-language-model service (including but not limited to public ChatGPT, Claude.ai, Google Translate's public web interface, DeepL's free web interface, Gemini, or similar) without the Company's prior written consent for the specific tool and the specific assignment. The Linguist acknowledges that such services may retain, train on, or further disclose any input submitted to them. Use of CAT-tool integrations and machine-translation engines that the Company has explicitly approved within the vendor portal is permitted.

(g) **No retention beyond the assignment.** Not retain Confidential Information after the Linguist's role in the relevant assignment has ended, except as permitted by Section 8.

(h) **Comply with privacy law.** Handle any personal information contained in Confidential Information in compliance with PIPEDA, the *Personal Information Protection Act* (Alberta), and any other applicable privacy or data-protection law, including by promptly reporting any actual or suspected privacy breach to the Company in accordance with Section 9.

## 6. Permitted disclosures

The Linguist may disclose Confidential Information only:

(a) to a sub-contractor or assistant whom the Company has approved **in writing in advance** for the specific assignment, and only after that sub-contractor has signed a confidentiality agreement on terms no less protective than this one; or

(b) where required by a court order, regulator, or applicable law, **provided that** the Linguist (i) gives the Company prompt written notice before disclosure where lawfully permitted to do so, (ii) discloses only the minimum information legally required, and (iii) cooperates with the Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy at the Company's expense.

## 7. Intellectual property

(a) **No licence.** Nothing in this agreement transfers ownership of Confidential Information to the Linguist or grants the Linguist any licence to use it beyond the limited use permitted by Section 5(b).

(b) **Work product.** All translations, revisions, post-edits, transcriptions, glossaries, term lists, translation memories, and other deliverables produced by the Linguist in connection with the Services are works made for hire for the Company (or, where the work-for-hire doctrine does not apply, are hereby assigned by the Linguist to the Company) and are themselves Confidential Information of the Company.

(c) **Moral rights.** To the extent permitted by law, the Linguist waives all moral rights in deliverables produced for the Company.

## 8. Return or destruction

Upon the completion of an assignment, the termination of the Linguist's engagement with the Company, or written request from the Company (whichever is earliest), the Linguist will, at the Company's option:

(a) return all Confidential Information in the Linguist's possession or control (including all copies, notes, and derivatives) to the Company through the vendor portal or another channel directed by the Company; or

(b) permanently destroy all such Confidential Information and provide the Company with written certification of destruction.

The Linguist may retain one copy of Confidential Information solely to the extent required by law or by the Linguist's professional obligations, in which case the obligations of this agreement continue to apply to that copy indefinitely.

## 9. Breach notification

If the Linguist becomes aware of any actual, suspected, or threatened unauthorized access to, disclosure of, loss of, or other breach involving Confidential Information, the Linguist will notify the Company in writing **without undue delay and in any event within 48 hours**, providing all reasonable detail about the incident and cooperating in good faith with the Company's investigation and remediation, including any notification obligations to affected individuals or regulators.

## 10. Term and survival

(a) This agreement takes effect when the Linguist accepts it through the vendor portal and continues for the duration of the Linguist's relationship with the Company.

(b) The Linguist's obligations under Sections 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12 **survive** the end of that relationship and continue for **five (5) years** thereafter, except that obligations relating to information that is a trade secret or personal information continue **indefinitely** for as long as such information remains a trade secret or personal information requiring protection under applicable law.

## 11. Remedies

The Linguist acknowledges that any breach of this agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Company and its clients for which damages alone would be inadequate, and that the Company is entitled, in addition to any other remedy available at law or equity, to seek injunctive or equitable relief without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting bond.

## 12. General

(a) **Independent contractor.** Nothing in this agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties. The Linguist is an independent contractor.

(b) **No exclusivity.** This agreement does not oblige the Company to provide any Services to the Linguist, nor does it oblige the Linguist to accept any particular assignment.

(c) **Entire agreement.** Together with any separate services agreement and assignment-specific instructions, this agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the confidentiality of Confidential Information and supersedes any prior understandings on that subject.

(d) **Amendment.** The Company may update this agreement from time to time by issuing a new version through the vendor portal. The Linguist's continued use of the vendor portal after being prompted to accept the new version, and after accepting it, constitutes agreement to the new version. The Company will retain a record of each version the Linguist has accepted and the date of acceptance.

(e) **Severability.** If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect, and the unenforceable provision will be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving its original intent.

(f) **No waiver.** Failure to enforce any right under this agreement is not a waiver of that right.

(g) **Assignment.** The Linguist may not assign this agreement. The Company may assign this agreement to an affiliate or to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

(h) **Governing law.** This agreement is governed by the laws of the Province of Alberta and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein, without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

(i) **Jurisdiction.** The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of the Province of Alberta sitting in Calgary for any dispute arising out of or relating to this agreement, except that the Company may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction.

(j) **Electronic acceptance.** The Linguist acknowledges that acceptance of this agreement through the vendor portal — by reading the agreement, checking the acknowledgement box, and clicking the acceptance button — constitutes the Linguist's electronic signature under the *Electronic Transactions Act* (Alberta) and PIPEDA, and produces a legally binding agreement equivalent to a handwritten signature. The Company will retain a record of the Linguist's acceptance, including the date and time, the version of the agreement accepted, the IP address from which acceptance was made, and other technical metadata, as evidence of agreement.

## Notes for the lawyer

Please review with particular attention to:

1. **Jurisdiction default.** Drafted for Alberta (Calgary). If Cethos Solutions Inc. is incorporated federally and operating in another province as its principal place of business, please correct the governing law and forum.
2. **PIPEDA / PIPA fit.** Section 3(b) and 5(h) reference both PIPEDA and Alberta PIPA. If Cethos handles personal information of Quebec residents at any scale, Law 25 obligations may need to flow through to the Linguist.
3. **Five-year survival.** Common but not universal. Some translation-industry NDAs set survival to three years; some to seven. Your call.
4. **Section 5(f) on AI tools.** This is the clause most likely to be challenged or revised — it's a 2024+ market-standard concern but the prohibition is intentionally strict. Decide whether you want to soften it (e.g., allow zero-retention enterprise tiers like ChatGPT Enterprise or DeepL Pro) or keep it absolute.
5. **Section 7(b) on work-for-hire.** Canadian copyright does not have a US-style "work for hire" doctrine for independent contractors — the assignment in the parenthetical is what actually does the work. Please confirm the assignment language is sufficient under the *Copyright Act* (Canada).
6. **Section 12(d) on amendments.** This is a unilateral-amendment clause. Common in clickwrap, but courts have struck them down where the changes are material and not separately accepted. Our flow re-prompts acceptance for each new version — please confirm that's enough.
7. **Reciprocity.** The current draft is one-way (Linguist owes confidentiality to Cethos). Consider whether a mutual NDA is more appropriate given that Linguists also share their CVs, sample work, and personal information with us.
8. **Indemnity / limitation of liability.** Not currently included — added these to a separate services agreement, or roll into this NDA?
9. **NDA text version + hash.** We will store the exact text and a SHA-256 hash of each accepted version. Please confirm the hash + version-id retention approach is acceptable as evidence of the text the Linguist saw.
